Why Milan?

Secrets of Innovation and Heritage

While Milan is often celebrated for fashion and finance, one of its best-kept secrets lies underground: the city hosts a vast network of hidden canals, or *navigli*, once engineered by Leonardo da Vinci himself. These waterways, now mostly covered or repurposed, were instrumental in transporting marble for the construction of the Duomo and played a major role in Milan's industrial growth. Today, remnants of this canal system can still be explored in the Navigli district, where ancient infrastructure quietly supports one of the city's most vibrant nightlife and art scenes.

Another little-known fact is Milan's role in contemporary urban agriculture. Beneath its modern skyline, the city is nurturing a green revolution with rooftop farms, vertical gardens, and innovative sustainability projects. Initiatives like the *Bosco Verticale* (Vertical Forest) — two residential towers covered in over 20,000 plants — are not just visually stunning, but also help reduce pollution and regulate temperature. These hidden layers of Milan reveal a city not just rich in history, but also deeply invested in shaping the future of urban living.
