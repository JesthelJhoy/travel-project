Why Milan?

Secrets of Innovation and Heritage

While Milan is often celebrated for fashion and finance, one of its best-kept secrets lies underground: the city hosts a vast network of hidden canals, or *navigli*, once engineered by Leonardo da Vinci himself. These waterways, now mostly covered or repurposed, were instrumental in transporting marble for the construction of the Duomo and played a major role in Milan's industrial growth. Today, remnants of this canal system can still be explored in the Navigli district, where ancient infrastructure quietly supports one of the city's most vibrant nightlife and art scenes.

Another little-known fact is Milan's role in contemporary urban agriculture. Beneath its modern skyline, the city is nurturing a green revolution with rooftop farms, vertical gardens, and innovative sustainability projects. Initiatives like the *Bosco Verticale* (Vertical Forest) — two residential towers covered in over 20,000 plants — are not just visually stunning, but also help reduce pollution and regulate temperature. These hidden layers of Milan reveal a city not just rich in history, but also deeply invested in shaping the future of urban living.

Aperitifs

My favorite Aperitifs in Milan

LúBar

A stylish and airy café housed in a historic greenhouse, LùBar blends Sicilian flavors with Milanese charm—perfect for everything from a relaxed breakfast to elegant aperitivo.

Address:

Via Palestro 16, Milan

What I like about it:

Tucked beside the Villa Reale gardens, LùBar is as beautiful as it is delicious. The vibe is serene yet sophisticated, and whether you're stopping by for a cappuccino, a Sicilian arancina, or a spritz at golden hour, it's always a treat.

Terrazza 12

A chic rooftop lounge perched above the city, offering panoramic views of Milan’s skyline along with expertly crafted cocktails in a sleek, retro-inspired setting.

Address:

Via Durini 28, Milan

What I like about it:

It’s one of the best spots in Milan for a stylish aperitivo with a view. Whether you're sipping a Negroni at sunset or enjoying a quiet drink under the stars, Terrazza 12 always feels like a hidden gem above the buzz of the city.

The Roof Milano

An elegant rooftop restaurant and bar with sweeping views over Piazza del Duomo—perfect for a refined lunch, sunset aperitivo, or a romantic dinner under the city lights.

Address:

Via Alberico Albricci 2–4, Milan

What I like about it:

The view is absolutely stunning—you feel like you can almost touch the Duomo. It's an ideal spot to unwind above the city, with a stylish atmosphere, great cocktails, and a menu that’s as impressive as the skyline.
